Kulfi

Ingredients: 1 liter full-fat milk 1/2 cup sugar 1/4 cup chopped nuts (such as almonds, pistachios, or cashews) 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder 2 tablespoons condensed milk


Instructions:
1.In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil. Then, reduce the heat to low and let the milk simmer, stirring occasionally, for about an hour or until it has reduced to half its original volume.
2.Add the sugar, chopped nuts, and cardamom powder to the milk, and stir well.
3.Cook the mixture for another 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture has thickened.
4.Add the condensed milk to the mixture and stir well. This will give the kulfi a creamy texture.
5.Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then pour it into kulfi molds or small cups.
6.Freeze the kulfi for at least 6 hours or overnight until it is completely frozen.
7.To serve, remove the kulfi from the molds or cups and garnish with additional chopped nuts, if desired.

Pav Bhaji

Ingredients: 4-5 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and boiled 1 large onion, chopped 1 large tomato, chopped 1 green bell pepper, chopped 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 2-3 tablespoons Pav Bhaji masala Salt to taste 2 tablespoons oil 2 tablespoons butter 4-5 Pav buns Fresh coriander leaves, chopped Lemon wedges


Instructions:
1.Mash the boiled potatoes using a potato masher or fork and keep them aside.
2.Heat oil in a pan and add chopped onions. Saute till the onions turn translucent.
3.Add ginger-garlic paste and saute for a few seconds.
4.Add chopped tomatoes and bell pepper and cook until they become soft.
5.Add Pav Bhaji masala, salt and mix well.
6.Add mashed potatoes and mix everything together.
7.Add a little water and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes on low flame.
8.Once the mixture is cooked well, add butter and mix it well.
9.In a separate pan, cut the Pav buns into halves and toast them on both sides until they are golden brown.
10.Serve the hot Pav Bhaji with toasted Pav buns, chopped coriander leaves, and lemon wedges.

Masala Dosa

Ingredients: 1 cup of rice 1/2 cup of urad dal 1/4 cup of chana dal 1/4 tsp of fenugreek seeds Salt to taste Oil Water

For the Masala: 3 boiled and mashed potatoes 1/2 chopped onion 2 chopped green chilies 1 tsp of mustard seeds 1 tsp of urad dal A pinch of asafoetida Curry leaves Salt to taste


Instructions: 1.Wash and soak the rice and urad dal in water for 6-7 hours. Also, soak chana dal and fenugreek seeds in water for 6-7 hours separately.
2.Drain the water and grind the rice and urad dal separately to a fine paste using a grinder or mixer. Mix both the pastes, add salt and water to make a thin batter. Cover and let it ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours.
3.To make the masala, heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, urad dal, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Once the mustard seeds start popping, add onions, green chilies, and fry until onions become translucent. Add boiled and mashed potatoes, salt, and mix well. Cook for a few minutes and set aside.
4.Heat a flat pan or tawa and grease it with oil. Pour a ladle full of the batter and spread it evenly in a circular motion. Drizzle oil on top and cook on medium flame until it turns golden brown.
5.Place a spoonful of masala in the center of the dosa and fold it from both sides. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
